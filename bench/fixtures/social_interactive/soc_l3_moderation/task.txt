A mini chat UI with moderation. Two messages are pre-seeded:

  m1 (You):   "Initial note — I'll update this shortly."
  m2 (Alice): "Typo in my last message, please ignore."

Each of your own messages has two buttons: "Edit" and "Delete".
(Other users' messages are read-only for you.)

Do these three actions in order:

  A. Post a new top-level message with EXACT text:
       Here's the update: ship in Q2.
     Use the compose box at the bottom and click "Send".

  B. Edit message `m1` — click its "Edit" button, change the text to
     EXACTLY:
       Initial note — updated plan below.
     Click "Save edit".

  C. Delete the NEW message you just posted in (A) — click its "Delete"
     button. (Deleting leaves a tombstone "[deleted]".)

A moderation counter at the bottom reads:
  "Messages: N — edited: X — deleted: Y"
After your three actions it should read:
  "Messages: 3 — edited: 1 — deleted: 1"
(Count includes the tombstone; that's what you want.)

Finish with done PASS that quotes BOTH:
  1. The counter line verbatim (the "Messages: 3 — edited: 1 — deleted: 1"
     part).
  2. The edited body of message m1 verbatim: the text
     `Initial note — updated plan below.`

Example format:
  `done PASS 'Messages: 3 — edited: 1 — deleted: 1 | m1 body: Initial
  note — updated plan below.'`

The edited-body sentence IS your critical evidence. If any action
rejects or a counter value differs, done FAIL.
